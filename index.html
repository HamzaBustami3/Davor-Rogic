laurionb:

Background: I'm about 50 years old, with a full-time job and am definitely not blessed with natural chess skill. I reached out to GM Rogic for lessons after about 6 months of playing online and trying to improve (yes, I am another person who watched "The Queen's Gambit") and have been working with him for over a year. At the time I was (am?) very much a novice player.
Davor focuses on chess strategy, not short-term tactics and that has been extremely educational. The first thing I learned that I wasn't really losing games because "my opponent found a tactic". Instead, I was not developing my pieces, positioning them poorly, wasting time with useless moves, making bad trades, or choosing bad attacking plans . These poor strategic choices would leading to positions where falling victim to a tactic was basically inevitable. On the other hand, I found that if I managed to develop my pieces, position them well, make purposeful moves, and choose reasonable attacking plans I started winning a lot more games and the tactics would be in my favor. Davor's approach of emphasizing "simple chess", not complex ideas and deep calculation makes the game accessible and learnable. I'm not going to threaten any master-level players with these concepts, but they are extremely effective at my level of play. I would compare Davor's teaching style in this area to the way Daniel Naroditsky explains chess in his youtube videos.
Davor also revamped my opening repertoire.
